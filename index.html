General Raymond T. Odierno

"a giant in military circles, dedicated first and always to the servicemembers he commanded and served alongside."

-Joseph Biden, 46th President of the United States, Statement on the Death of Former Army Chief of Staff General Raymond T. Odierno, USA (Ret.)

Here's a time line of General Odierno's life:

  • Born - Rockaway, New Jersey on 08 September 1954
  • Died - Pinehurst, North Carolina on 08 October 2021
  • Education
    - Graduated the United States Military Academy at West Point with a Bachelor of Science.
    - Masters of Science in Nuclear Engineering from North Carolina State University.
    - Master of Arts Degree in national security and strategy from the Naval War College.
  • Career
    - Chief of Staff of the United States Army
    - Commander, United States Joint Forces Command, Norfolk, Virginia
    - Commander, United States Forces – Iraq, Operation Iraqi Freedom, Iraq
    - Commander, United States Forces – Iraq, Operation Iraqi Freedom, Iraq
    - Commander, United States Forces – Iraq, Operation Iraqi Freedom, Iraq
    - Commanding General, III Corps and Commander, Multi-National Corps-Iraq, Operation Iraqi Freedom, Iraq
    - Commanding General, III Corps and Commander, Multi-National Corps-Iraq, Operation Iraqi Freedom, Iraq
    - Assistant to the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, Office of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, Washington, DC
    - Special Assistant to Vice Chief of Staff, United States Army, Washington, DC
    - Commanding General, 4th Infantry Division (Mechanized), Fort Hood, Texas. Operation Iraqi Freedom, Iraq
    - Director, Force Management, Office of the Deputy Chief of Staff for Operations and Plans, United States Army, Washington, DC
    - Assistant Division Commander (Support), 1st Armored Division, United States Army Europe and Seventh Army, Germany to include duty as Deputy Commanding General for Ground Operations, Task Force Hawk, Operation Allied Force, Albania
    - Chief of Staff, V Corps, United States Army Europe and Seventh Army, Germany
    - Commander, Division Artillery, 1st Cavalry Division, Fort Hood, Texas

"To build trust between soldiers and leaders there must be a leader who believes in what they are doing; a leader who leads from the front; a leader who is able then to garner a respect that ultimately engenders trust from his subordinates and his fellow leaders. Trust goes beyond the faith soldiers have in each other and includes trust between the Army and soldiers.

--General Odierno, Institute of Land Warfare Military Forum titled "The Army Profession" Oct. 23 during the Association of the United States Army’s Annual Meeting and Exposition
